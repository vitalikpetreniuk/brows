Overview

What is Microblading?

Microblading is a form of tattooing, but it uses thin blades that deposit pigment into tiny incisions rather than a traditional tattoo gun. Due to differences between the tools and the less permanent nature of pigment compared to ink, microblade coloring typically lasts up to three years.

As a semi-permanent solution that can be tailored to each individual client, microblading appeals to many people who would rather not spend time and money worrying over eyebrow pencils day after day. The results are natural looking designs that mimic the shade and shape of your own eyebrow hair.

How it works

  • Step 1

    Your technician will measure your brows and then sketch the shape of your eyebrows with makeup pencil. Based on the unique shape of your face and your preferences and instructions, the artist will draw a washable version of what your semi-permanent pigmentation will look like.

  • Step 2

    With the initial designs confirmed and the color selected, your technician will apply a numbing cream to the affected areas which reduces or eliminates pain provoked in the microblading process. In most cases numbing cream is applied only once at the beginning of the process, but if you have especially sensitive skin it can be reapplied as necessary.

  • Step 3

    Using a special microblading tool, your technician will draw your semi-permanent eyebrows over the original washable designs. This is the true art of microblading, and requires specialized equipment and skill. After the microblading is complete the affected areas may swell and feel tender, but this is normal and temporary, so don’t let it concern you.

  • Step 4

    Allowing your microblade eyebrows to properly heal is an important step in the process. Once the healing is complete, any necessary touch-ups can be applied. If you feel like the initial coloring is too dark, don’t worry; the pigment will naturally lighten over time before eventually fading. If it progresses to a shade that you particularly like, remember to take pictures so you can show your technician what you want at future appointments.
